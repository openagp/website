If you run AI agents across multiple vendors, AGP lets you **author one policy** and have it enforced everywhere — with a single, signed, verifiable audit trail.

## One policy, every vendor

Policy is declarative YAML. The plane signs it and pushes it to each conformant vendor, which applies it and stamps the decision on every event.

```yaml
agp_policy_version: "0.1"
policy_id: "acme-prod-policies"
issuer: "acme.example.com"
applies_to:
  vendors: ["*"]
  agents: ["*"]
rules:
  - id: rule_pii_outbound
    when:
      action.type: tool_call
      action.target_resource:
        domain_not_in: ["acme.com", "*.acme.com"]
      action.input_summary:
        contains_pattern: "ssn|credit_card|email"
    then:
      decision: blocked
      reason: "PII outbound to external domain"
  - id: rule_log_db_writes
    when:
      action.tool_name:
        starts_with: "database.write"
    then:
      decision: logged_only
      annotate:
        scf_controls: ["DATA-08", "AUDIT-12"]
```

## What you get

- **One audit view.** Every vendor's events arrive in one canonical, signed format — no per-vendor log parsing.
- **Proof of enforcement.** Each event carries the `policy_hash` that was in force, so you can demonstrate *which* policy version applied to *which* action.
- **Verifiable provenance.** Events are Ed25519-signed; your ledger is hash-chained and independently verifiable — you don't have to take a vendor's word.
- **Procurement leverage.** Require a minimum [conformance level](/learn/conformance/) in your RFPs; discover conformant vendors in the [registry](/registry/).

## Choosing conformance levels per workload

Use **L1** vendors for observability, **L2** for in-vendor policy enforcement, and prefer **L3** vendors for your most sensitive, high-stakes workloads where actions must be gated in real time.
