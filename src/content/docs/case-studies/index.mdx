Abstract protocols are easy to nod along to and hard to feel. These are concrete, recognizable situations a regulated enterprise actually faces — and how AGP changes the outcome. Each walks the full path: the situation, what breaks *without* a standard, the AGP messages on the wire, and what an auditor can independently verify afterward.

  <LinkCard title="Surviving an EU AI Act audit (auditor)" href="/case-studies/eu-ai-act-audit/" description="A compliance lead must evidence human oversight and automated enforcement. With AGP it's a query over signed events, not a quarter of screenshot-gathering." />
  <LinkCard title="Investigating a cross-vendor incident (lineage)" href="/case-studies/multi-vendor-incident/" description="One bad outcome touched three vendors. Shared trace and lineage turn three incompatible logs into a single, ordered, verifiable story." />
  <LinkCard title="Human-approving a wire transfer (L3)" href="/case-studies/realtime-approval/" description="A finance agent proposes a high-value payment. L3 gates it on a human decision in real time — and proves the human approved before money moved." />
</CardGrid>
