## The AGP primitives that become evidence

Five protocol features do the work in every mapping below:

- **Signed canonical event** — every agent action, attributable to a registered key. Forgery requires key compromise.
- **`policy.decision` + `policy_hash`** — proof that a specific policy version governed a specific action (`allowed` / `blocked` / `logged_only`).
- **L3 decision** — a high-stakes action gated on a synchronous (often human) decision *before* it executes.
- **Hash-chained ledger** — tamper-evident ordering; insertion, deletion, or reordering is detectable.
- **`lineage`** — `trace_id` / `parent_event_id` reconstruct a workflow across vendors.

## AIUC-1

[AIUC-1](https://aiuc.com/) is a certification standard for enterprise AI agents — "SOC 2 for AI agents" — covering six domains. AGP supplies runtime evidence for the operational ones; **AIUC-1 certifies, AGP proves continuously.**

| AIUC-1 domain | AGP evidence |
|---|---|
| **Data & privacy** | Policy rules that block PII egress to external domains; events carry `input_hash`/`output_hash` (commitments, not payloads); `human_principal` is a salted hash, never plaintext. |
| **Security** | Ed25519-signed events verified against the registry; in-vendor enforcement of tool/target restrictions; mTLS transport; algorithm-substitution-resistant signing (ADR 0001). |
| **Safety** | L3 real-time gating of high-stakes actions; `blocked` decisions recorded; fallback **fails closed** and the event records that it fired. |
| **Reliability** | Deterministic, reproducible policy evaluation; declared decision budget and fallback behavior captured per event. |
| **Accountability** | Hash-chained ledger; per-event `policy_hash`; `lineage`; signatures any third party can verify without trusting the plane or vendor. |
| **Societal risks** | Policy encodes use/content restrictions; `logged_only` enables monitoring without blocking; a durable, auditable record supports oversight. |

## Cross-framework matrix

The same evidence anchors to the controls regulators and auditors already ask for. Anchors are indicative, at the control-family level.

| AGP evidence | AIUC-1 | EU AI Act | NIST AI RMF | ISO/IEC 42001 | SOC 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Signed canonical event | Accountability | Art. 12 — record-keeping / automatic logs | MEASURE (monitor) | Logging & monitoring (Annex A) | CC7 — system monitoring |
| `policy_hash` + decision | Security · Accountability | Art. 12 · Art. 9 (risk controls) | MANAGE | Operational controls | CC6 — logical access |
| L3 decision / human gate | Safety | Art. 14 — human oversight | GOVERN · MANAGE | Human oversight controls | CC5 — control activities |
| Hash-chained ledger | Accountability | Art. 12 (traceability) | MEASURE | Evidence integrity | CC7 / audit logging |
| Conformance report (CTS) | (vendor attestation) | Art. 15 — accuracy & cybersecurity | MAP | Supplier/AI-component assurance | CC9 — vendor management |

<Aside type="caution" title="What this is and isn't">
These mappings are **guidance for assembling evidence**, not a claim of certification. AGP does not certify a vendor or grant a SOC 2 / AIUC-1 attestation — accredited auditors do that. AGP makes the evidence those audits need a *by-product of normal operation* rather than a manual project. Confirm exact clause applicability with your auditor.
</Aside>

## How to use this

- **Vendors** pursuing AIUC-1 or SOC 2: emit AGP events at L1/L2 and your accountability, security, and monitoring evidence is generated and signed automatically — hand the auditor verifiable records, not screenshots.
- **Customers / auditors**: query the ledger by control area, verify signatures against the registry, and confirm the `policy_hash` chain — see the [EU AI Act audit case study](/case-studies/eu-ai-act-audit/).
- **Insurers**: verifiable runtime telemetry (what was attempted, what was enforced, what failed closed) is a stronger basis for pricing agent-failure risk than self-attestation.
