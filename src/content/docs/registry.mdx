The **AGP Registry** is a public, decentralized directory of conformant actors — vendors, planes, and customers — and their public keys. It is the source of truth a plane uses to verify signatures before trusting a single byte.

## How the registry works

- Each actor is **one signed JSON file** at `actors/<fqdn>.json`, validated against [`schemas/actor.json`](https://github.com/openagp/spec/blob/main/schemas/actor.json).
- Entries are **self-signed** (RFC 8785 JCS + Ed25519, per [ADR 0001](/spec/adr-0001/)). A merge attests that the entry is well-formed and its conformance report verifies — **not** an endorsement of the actor's product.
- Anyone can **mirror** the registry; verification works without the central index.

## Submitting your entry

<LinkButton href="https://github.com/openagp/registry#submitting-an-entry" icon="external">Submit an entry</LinkButton>

1. Run [`agp-cts`](https://github.com/openagp/cts) against your endpoint to produce a signed conformance report.
2. Copy [`actors/example.openagp.io.json`](https://github.com/openagp/registry/blob/main/actors/example.openagp.io.json) to `actors/<your-fqdn>.json` and fill in your identity, keys, endpoints, and conformance report.
3. Sign it with one of your advertised keys, then open a PR.
4. A maintainer verifies the report signature, schema validity, and endpoint reachability, then merges.

Registry data is published under **CC0** — the index is public domain; the signed claims inside each entry remain authored by their submitters.
